1. PSD

M is positive semidefinite matrix ⟺ all principal submatrices P of M are PSD

Note: This follows by considering the quadratic form xTMx and looking at the components of x corresponding to the defining subset of principal submatrix. The converse is trivially true.

3. Duality

Two equivalent ways to represent a convex set:

  • standard representation: The family of points in the set
  • dual representation: The set of halfspaces containing the set (半平面的交集)

A closed convex set S is the intersection of all closed halfspaces H containing it.

Polar Let S⊆Rn be a convex set containing the origin. The polar of S is defined as follows

S∘:={y ∣ yTx≤1, ∀x∈S}

Note

  • polar is one way of representing the all halfspaces containing a convex set
  • every halfspace aTx≤b with b=0 can be written as a “normalized” inequality yTx≤1, by dividing by b
  • S∘ can be thought of as the normalized representations of halfspaces containing S

Properties of the polar:

  1. S∘∘=S
  2. S∘ is a closed convex set containing the origin
  3. When 0 is in the interior of S, then S∘ is bounded
  4. When S is non-convex, S∘=(conv(S))∘, and S∘∘=conv(S)

  2. K∘ is closed and convex

Conjugation of convex functions Let f:Rn↦R∪{∞} be a convex function. The conjugation of f is

f^*(y) := \sup_\limits{x}(y^Tx - f(x))

Properties of the conjugate

  1. f∗∗=f
  2. f∗ is convex (supremum of affine functions of y)

7. Strong convexity

A differentiable function f is strongly convex if

f(y)≥f(x)+∇f(x)T(y−x)+2μ​∥y−x∥2

Note

  1. f is not necessarily differentiable, (see the equivalent definition)
  2. if f is non-smooth, gradient → subgradient
  3. strong convexity ⟹ strict convexity

Note: Intuitively speaking, strong convexity means that there exists a quartic lower bound on the growth of the function.

Equivalent definition

​(i) f(y)≥f(x)+∇f(x)T(y−x)+2μ​∥y−x∥2, ∀x,y.(ii) g(x)=f(x)−2μ​∥x∥2 is convex, ∀x.(iii) ⟨∇f(x)−∇f(y),x−y⟩≥μ∥x−y∥2, ∀x,y.(iv) f(αx+(1−α)y)≤αf(x)+(1−α)f(y)−2α(1−α)μ​∥x−y∥2, α∈[0,1].(v) ∇2f(x)⪰μI​​

Tagent cone Let M be a (nonempty) convex set and x∗∈M, the tagent cone of M at x∗ is the cone

TM​(x∗)​={h∈Rn∣x∗+th∈M,∀t>0}={y∈Rn ∣ y−x∗∈M}​

Note:

  • Geometrically, this is the set of all directions leading from x∗ inside M
  • convex but not necessarily closed
  • fact: if x∗ is a minimizer, then ∀h∈TM​(x∗)⟹hT∇f(x∗)≥0. （因为tangent cone里面都是可行解，所以必须不是下降方向）
  • TM​(x∗)=Rn⟺x∗∈intM

e.g. 多面体

M={x∣Ax≤b}={x∣aiT​x≤bi​,i=1,…,m}

the tangent cone at x∗ is

TM​(x∗)={h ∣ aiT​h≤0, ∀i, aiT​x∗=bi​}

Normal cone: the polar cone of tangent cone

NM​(x∗)={g∈Rn ∣ ⟨g,y−x∗⟩≤0, ∀y∈M}

Note:

  • normal cone is the polar to tangent cone, i.e.,
TM​(x∗)NM​(x∗)​={g∈Rn ∣ ⟨g,y−x∗⟩≥0, ∀y∈M}={g∈Rn ∣ ⟨g,y−x∗⟩≤0, ∀y∈M}​
  • fact: if x∗ is a minimizer, then −∇f(x∗)∈NM​(x∗).
